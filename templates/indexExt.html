Increased Attention and Concentration

Learning another language improves Executive functions of the brain.

These are tasks involved with your ability to control, direct and manage your attention, as well as your ability to plan. It also helps you ignore irrelevant information and focus on what's important.


Helps avoid Distractions

Delay illness like Alzheimer's and Dementia

Although learning a language may not totally halt the onset of Alzheimer's and other forms of dementia in susceptible individuals, it can fend it off for up to five years. This is much more effective than any currently prescribed drug.

Denser Grey Matter

Multilingual individuals have a greater density of grey matter.

This increases the brain's plasticity and ability to grasp new information.


Increases Cognition

Multilinguals have sharper cognitive skills and keep their brain alert and active even when only one language is used

Easier to learn New Languages

Once your brain has been trained to spot patterns involved in learning a new language, it makes it much easier for it to spot the patterns in the future.

Learning a language focuses on grammar and structures, making multilinguals more aware of language overall. This makes them more effective communicators, editors and writers.
